The history of biathlon in Ukraine

The biathlon in Ukraine began to develop at the end of the 1950s in Chernihiv region at the sports club Kolos. In the 1960s, development continued in the Dynamo community of Sumy and Kharkiv regions. In 1961 a biathlon section was organized at the Kyiv State Institute of Physical Culture and Sports, which included the skiers of the Institute - Anatoly Studionov, Mykola Bleschunov, Yakov Dvorsky, Volodymyr Krushinsky, Viktor Kradenov, Pavel Verstakov, Leonid Tkachuk, Vasyl Lomayev, Stanislav Pokanevych. These athletes became members of the first national team of Ukraine on biathlon.

Ivan Surovtsev became the first Ukrainian athlete to take part in the 1968 Winter Olympics in Grenoble (France). Master of Sports of the USSR of the international class Vasyl Karlenko in 1973-1976 became the laureate of the international competitions of the championships of the USSR at various distances. 1974 - Ukrainian national team won in the race race in the most prestigious All-Union competitions "Izhevsk rifle". Ivan Byakov became the champion of the 1972 Winter Olympics in Sapporo and in 1976 in Innsbruck. Alexander Rostopin became the champion and prize-winner of the USSR championships in 1974-1976 and took fourth place in the 1977 World Championship. Peter Bruyenko won the title of the champion of the II Winter Sports Games of the USSR in 1982 in a race for 20 kilometers. Taras Dolny - won three gold medals at the World Winter Universiade in Borovets (Bulgaria) in 1983. Nadia Bilova is a gold medal in redirection and silver in a sprint race at the 1986 World Cup in Falun, Sweden. Alexander Rostopin won the fourth place in the world championship. Natalia Prikazchikova became the champion and bronze medalist of the 1989 World Cup. Antonina Sokirko (Serik) - won three gold medals of the USSR championships.

Successes at the World Championships

It is worth noting Oksana Khvostenko, Vita and Valya Semerenko, who won silver medals at the 2008 World Cup Relay.

At the 2011 World Cup, the Ukrainian team won three awards: the bronze medal of Vita Semerenko, the bronze medals (Sergey Sednev, Andriy Derizemlya, Sergey Semenov, Alexander Bilanenko), and silver awards for female relay race (Valya Semerenko, Vita Semerenko, Olena Pidgrushna, Oksana Khvostenko ) But the doping test of Oksana Khvostenko gave a positive result to ephedrine and the medal was taken away. As a result, the silver was received by the team of France, and the bronze - Belarus.

In the 2013 World Cup, the Ukrainian team won five awards: gold (Olena Podgrusna in sprint), silver (relay in the composition of Julia Jim, Vita Semerenko, Valya Semerenko, Elena Podgrusna) and three bronze medals (Olena Pidgrushna in the race for persecution, Vita Semerenko in the sprint, Valya Semerenko in an individual race).

At the World Cup 2015, Ukraine won one gold and one bronze medal. Valya Semerenko won gold at mass start and bronze at the sprint

At the 2016 World Cup, Ukraine won one bronze medal: Sergey Semyonov at the sprint.
