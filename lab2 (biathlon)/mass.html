Mass start

Mass Starta (or the race from a general start) is a kind of biathlon race at 15 km for men, 12.5 km for women and juniors, 10 km for juniors and boys, and 7.5 km for girls with four firing lines. It consists of five laps of 3 km for men, 2.5 km for women and juniors, 2 km for juniors and boys, and 1.5 km for girls. Biathletes (as a rule - 30 best athletes in the framework of various biathlon competitions) start simultaneously from a general start. The first and second shooting - lying, the third and fourth - standing. Biathletes occupy places on the firing line in accordance with the order of arrival at the shooting range (on the first - in accordance with the order of the starting number). For each mistake, a penalty circle of 150 m is provided.

Starting with the 2011/12 World Cup, new rules on the admission of athletes to the mass start come into force. If previously 30 top athletes of the World Cup overall standings were allowed, now 25 athletes are admitted to the race according to the World Cup overall standings, as well as 5 athletes who are not in this number, but who have scored the most points for the current stage. At the Olympic Games and World Championships in the mass start, all the winners of the current Games (Championship) in the sprint, pursuit and individual race, the top 15 in the overall World Cup, the rest (from 6 to 15 people) get to the points at the current Olympic Games. games (world championships).

In the World Cup is a separate record for this discipline. As a result of the season, the winners among men and women receive the Small Crystal Globe.
